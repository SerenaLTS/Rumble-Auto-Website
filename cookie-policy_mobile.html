Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how RUMBLE Auto may use cookies and similar technologies to operate our website, measure performance and improve the browsing experience.

Effective date: April 16, 2026

1. What Cookies Are

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit, support core functionality and provide information about how the site is used.

2. How We Use Cookies

RUMBLE Auto may use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  • to operate and secure the website
  • to remember basic preferences and improve the browsing experience
  • to understand which pages visitors view and how they navigate the site
  • to measure website performance, campaign performance and enquiry activity
  • to help diagnose technical issues and prevent misuse

3. Types Of Cookies We May Use

Our website may use the following categories of cookies.

  • Essential cookies: required for basic site operation, security and page navigation.
  • Preference cookies: used to remember choices such as cookie consent or display preferences.
  • Analytics cookies: used to understand traffic, engagement and website performance.
  • Marketing or measurement cookies: used to assess the effectiveness of campaigns or understand lead activity where enabled.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies or similar technologies may be provided by third-party services that help us operate the website, including analytics, maps, embedded media, hosting, form handling or performance measurement tools. These providers may process technical information according to their own policies.

5. Analytics And Performance Measurement

Where enabled, analytics tools may collect information such as pages viewed, approximate location, device type, browser type, referral source, time on page and interactions with links or forms. This information helps us improve the site and understand how visitors engage with our products and services.

6. Managing Cookies

You can usually manage or block cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to delete existing cookies, block future cookies or receive a warning before cookies are stored. If you disable cookies, some website features may not work as intended.

7. Cookie Consent

Where a cookie consent tool is available, you can use it to accept or reject non-essential cookies. Essential cookies may still be used because they are necessary for the website to function properly.

8. Personal Information

Some cookie or analytics information may be considered personal information if it can identify or reasonably be linked to an individual. For more details about how we handle personal information, please read our Privacy Policy.

9. Changes To This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our website, tools, business operations or legal requirements. The updated version will be published on this page with a revised effective date.
